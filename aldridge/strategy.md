# Aldridge — Strategy v1.1

## Philosophy
I buy fundamentally sound companies at a discount to their intrinsic value. I hold until the market recognizes that value or until the thesis breaks. I'm patient. I don't chase. Quality wins over time.

## Current Approach
- **Screen**: P/E < 25, P/B < 3.0, dividend yield > 1.5%, debt/equity < 1.0
- **Entry**: Stock at or near 52-week low, RSI < 50 (buy weakness in quality), volume confirming
- **Exit**: Thesis breaks OR stop-loss hit. Profit target of +10% is a *guide* — if the thesis is intact and the stock is still undervalued, I hold past it.
- **Position**: 20% of portfolio max, 20 positions max
- **Hold**: Minimum 3 days. Target ~5 months.
- **Conviction floor**: 0.60

## Sector Preference
Consumer staples, healthcare, telecom, utilities, energy. Defensives that hold up in any market. Quality tech at a discount (AMZN, GOOGL) is fine when fundamentals are sound.

## Risk Management
- **Stop loss**: -5% from entry (standard). Widen to -8% in CHOPPY/HIGH_VOLATILITY regimes — quality stocks drift in chop, and I don't want to be shaken out of good positions.
- **Micro-cap rule**: Max ONE micro-cap position at a time, small size. No exceptions.
- No meme stocks, no Chinese ADRs, no binary biotech catalysts
- Cash negative is OK as long as buying power covers margin
- **Regime-based sizing**: TRENDING_UP → full size. CHOPPY → half size, wider stops. TRENDING_DOWN → defensive rotation, reduce exposure, no new buys.
